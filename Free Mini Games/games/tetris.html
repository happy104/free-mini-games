About Tetris

Tetris is one of the most iconic puzzle games of all time. In this addictive, simple yet challenging game, your goal is to arrange falling tetrominoes (geometric shapes made up of four square blocks) to create complete rows. When a row is completed, it disappears, and you earn points. As the game progresses, the pieces fall faster, making it increasingly difficult to position them correctly. The game ends when the stack of tetrominoes reaches the top of the playing field.

Features

  • Classic tetromino shapes: I, J, L, O, S, T, and Z
  • Progressive difficulty levels
  • Score tracking and high score system
  • Line clear animations
  • Next piece preview
  • Hold piece functionality
  • Clean, modern interface

Controls

  • ← Move left
  • → Move right
  • ↓ Soft drop
  • SPACE Hard drop
  • ↑ Rotate clockwise
  • Z Rotate counter-clockwise
  • C Hold piece
  • P Pause game

Touch controls are also available for mobile devices.

Tetris Trivia

  • Tetris was created by Russian software engineer Alexey Pajitnov in 1984.
  • The name "Tetris" combines "tetra" (meaning four) and "tennis" (Pajitnov's favorite sport).
  • It's one of the best-selling video games of all time, with over 202 million copies sold.
  • The Tetris theme song is based on a Russian folk song called "Korobeiniki".
  • Scientists have discovered the "Tetris Effect," where people start to see falling blocks in their mind after playing for extended periods.
